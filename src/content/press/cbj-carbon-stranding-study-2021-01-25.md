The Charlotte Business Journal covered the launch of Tyler's Carbon Stranding
report, highlighting its central finding that Duke Energy's planned generation
investments could impose up to $4.8 billion in stranded asset costs on
ratepayers in North and South Carolina. The piece ran alongside similar
coverage by E&E News, Utility Dive, and Seeking Alpha on the same day.
