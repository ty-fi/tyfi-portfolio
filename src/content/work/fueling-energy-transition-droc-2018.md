A University of Michigan Sustainability Cases publication examining the
technical and economic feasibility of using renewable energy resources to
electrify the community of Beni in the Democratic Republic of Congo. The
case study evaluated solar, biomass, and hybrid configurations and modeled
the economics under various financing and demand scenarios.

The analysis emerged from Tyler's master's thesis at the University of Michigan
School of Natural Resources and the Environment: *Fueling a Transition:
Evaluating the Feasibility for a Hybrid Renewable Microgrid in Beni,
Democratic Republic of Congo* (2018).
