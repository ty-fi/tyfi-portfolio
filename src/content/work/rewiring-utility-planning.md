RMI analysis of the structural reforms needed in utility integrated resource
planning (IRP) processes to handle accelerating load growth. The report
examines the limitations of current planning frameworks — often designed
around flat or declining demand — and proposes a more dynamic, transparent,
and scenario-driven approach to capital deployment, transmission expansion,
and distributed energy resource integration.

The work draws on RMI's deep engagement with utility commissions across the
Southeast, Mountain West, and Mid-Atlantic regions.
