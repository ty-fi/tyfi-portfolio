Direct testimony filed with the North Carolina Utilities Commission in
docket E-100, Sub 179, addressing Duke Energy's 2022 Carbon Plan and
integrated resource plan. The testimony evaluated the plan's alignment
with North Carolina's House Bill 951 carbon reduction requirements and
the public interest, including analysis of clean energy deployment
trajectories, cost impacts on ratepayers, and risks to grid reliability.

Filed: September 2, 2022
Before: North Carolina Utilities Commission
On behalf of: Vote Solar
